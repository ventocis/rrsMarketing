## Which path are you on?

### I got a ticket in **Florida**
1) **Elect BDI within 30 days** with the **issuing county clerk** (check your citation).  
2) **Complete an FLHSMV-approved BDI** online.  
3) **Submit/verify** your completion with the **same clerk** by their deadline (often **60–90 days**).

**When Road Ready is easiest:** You want **Spanish** or **English** BDI, **plain checklists** for the clerk, no certificate paywall, and a **mobile-first** flow.

### I got a ticket in **Michigan**
1) Wait for **Michigan SOS** to notify you that you’re **eligible**.  
2) You then have **60 days** to **enroll, complete, and pass BDIC** with an **approved sponsor**.  
3) **Sponsors report** your pass to SOS; keep your receipt.

**When Road Ready is easiest:** You need **clear 60-day planning**, **mobile study**, and reassurance about **points/insurer visibility** after you pass.

## Quick FAQs

**Does Michigan BDIC hide my ticket from insurers if I pass within 60 days?**  
When **SOS says you’re eligible** and you **pass within 60 days** with an **approved sponsor**, **points aren’t added** and the ticket is **not made available to insurers**. See Michigan SOS BDIC.

**If I already paid my Florida ticket, can I still elect BDI?**  
In many counties, paying **without** electing BDI waives the option. Contact the **issuing county clerk** immediately.

**How many times can I take Florida BDI?**  
Florida has **frequency limits** (e.g., once every 12 months; lifetime limits apply). Check FLHSMV and your **county** rules.

**What’s the difference between FL BDI and MI BDIC?**  
**Florida BDI** is something you **elect with the clerk** (then submit your certificate). **Michigan BDIC** requires **SOS eligibility** and a **60-day pass** with an approved sponsor who reports your completion.
